It's honestly surreal that my junior year will almost be over. Before I know it, I'll be writing my college apps, actually *going* to college, and deciding what the hell I'll actually do as an adult. Hopefully, AI doesn't completely replace the tech industry by then. 

Well, ok. That's enough daydreaming. The bottom line is that I have a *lot* of stuff to do from now until June, most of which changed from when I posted my spring break updates. So here's a bit of an update on my life (if you care): 

### 1. Piano
Even though I said that I'm lowkey retired from piano two months ago (admittedly robotics kinda took away that enthusiasm), it looks like I'm back on the grind. I guess emphasizing music as a big part of my profile is a much wiser strategy than applying to pure CS/Engineering, since there is literally no way I'm going up against USAMO/USACO Camp/ISEF Grand Award kids with my current profile. Of course, college apps and major awards aren't everything. As simply a person, I would hate to be a robot that doesn't know much aside from writing for-loops and inverting binary trees. 

As of writing this post, I have a casual school performance in two days, a formal studio recital the same Saturday, MSM juries two weeks after (final exam for music school), and eventually an arts portfolio I'm submitting to certain schools that offer it 💀. The first three are simply meant to be preparation for my portfolio, so I'm luckily not changing pieces in between. 

Right now, my intended pieces for my arts portfolio are: Bach's Keyboard Partita No. 2 in C Minor (Only the 1st movement), Liszt's Tarantella (from Venezia e Napoli), Beethoven's Sonata Op. 31 No. 3, and George Gershwin's Three Preludes. It's a fairly long program, but suprisingly managable imo. The Liszt is probably the technically hardest piece in this program, but nothing some slow practice on the chord spam/repeated note sections can't tackle; Admittedly, I'm not super experienced with Jazz music, but the preludes are short/easy enough for me to learn them fairly quickly; I've also played enough Beethoven and Bach to have an idea of how to approach them, though I'll say that the sonata is definetly harder than I expected. 

### 2. School
AP Exams... Since my school is really snobbish about classes, this is the first year I'm taking any AP exam. Right now, I'm taking APUSH, Calc BC, CSA, and Physics CMech, aiming for all 5s. CS and Calc are pretty easy and not classes I'm too worried about, although I definetly need to study for the other two—I'm trash at history, and my physics class is... not ran super well to say the least. My English teacher threw me another Socratic Seminar, so I have to prepare for both simultaneously which is... fun. 

Oh yeah, it turns out that I got an A on the physics test that I wrote a whole blog contemplating about. I'm pretty sure my classmates all got cooked on the FRQs, so I think I got saved by the curve. This probably shows that, once again, I am ridiculously paranoid, but I guess it's also better to worry earlier and frontload than cram everything three days before the exam. 

### 3. ECs
Oof... I'm kinda in a pickle right now. Slowly but surely, I've recieved decisions for summer programs, and so far, I haven't gotten into any of them. Granted, I'm at a disadvantage when it comes to summer programs for not being a US Citizen/Permanent resident, but it still sucks that I'm getting flat out rejected when I spent a decent amount of time on my essays. Oh well, that's life; you don't get everything you want.  

After some more thinking, it also turns out that venturing away from CBIO probably is not a good idea; both ideas I hinted at last time seem like dead-end research topics. In particular, applying AI to pretty much all facets of music seems like a really contrived usecase, and not something I feel passionate about working on. One thing I find really neat about CBIO is its versatility: there are a lot of really unique applications of CS (from very fancy frameworks for diagnosing cancer to designing algorithms to DNA sequencing) that are both practical and thought provoking. Also, I've already worked on a CBIO project, so it makes me to continue down the same direction instead of pivoting to another research topic. I guess a reason I was so adamant about switching was because of my TNYC results, but, looking back, that doesn't justifying outright giving up on CBIO. Don't let your emotions take over kids...

I also started working on robotics offseason, albeit at a very leisure pace. So far, I've mostly implemeted some extra auto features (namely predictive braking) and looked into PedroPathing's new Command Paradigm called Ivy. A big issue that's worrying me is who will be programmer once I graduate. I stumbled upon my school's 10th grade Python class page (mb for stalking) and found a few people who seem pretty qualified for software, but now the challenge is convincing them to spend time on the robot. After all, robotics is a *huge* commitment, bigger than any sports or other team at my school, so it's imperative that I frame robotics in a way that's engaging to them.  

### 4. Other stuff
Alright, enough of me waffling about academics. I was actually rather unproductive during spring break, but in turn stumbled across some absolute internet gems. 

**Music**
[Connect The World, Nanatsukaze](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M-IayQIR0XA) - I'm suprised this song doesn't have more views. The music video has to be the cutest thing I've seen all month. 

[It Ain't Over Till It's Over, Lenny Kravitz](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TmENMZFUU_0) - If life had an anthem, this would be the perfect song. Indeed, you never know what you'll end up becoming until it's over. Before then, keep working at it! 

**Movies**
*A Silent Voice (2016)* - Holy shit, this movie is amazing. Having gone through two years of pretty bad anxiety myself, this film *really* spoke to me. The characters feel incredibly real, and I found myself empathizing with both Shoko and Shoya. In fact, I even attempted to write my own analysis for this movie on my blog, but I've yet to finish it because I feel like my writing doesn't do this movie justice. Nonetheless, I highly recommend it. 

*Wolf Children (2012)* - This is a shockingly poignant coming-of-age story that illustrates the power of maternal love. I lowkey teared up realizing how much the mom went through for her two children. Living with my mom alone for the past three years (dad is in China for work reasons), I feel like I often take for granted how much she sacraficed. This movie is a firm reminder that I should probably give her a hug... love you mom 😘

Jesus christ I'm such an otaku now; I really need to find some western movies to watch. I want to watch Dune 3 and The Odessey when those come out, but for now, I'll be sticking to hand drawn cartoons coming straight out of Japan 😭. More reviews coming out soon!!
