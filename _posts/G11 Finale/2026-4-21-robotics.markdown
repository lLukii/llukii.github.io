Ever since early March, I've felt very salty about what felt like this Robotics season's abrupt and unfortuneate end. In fact, after getting some insider information from both AM and MT in the days following champs, it turns out the Ultro was on track for 3rd place inspire at one point (which could've very well solidified us a spot at worlds). Instead, a measily 10 lines of code changes ended up breaking our entire auto during the very matches that both judges and scouters watched. Subsequently, we walked away empty-handed without even making playoffs or any significant awards. The cherry on top was the FIRST leadership award finalist announcement: I was 4th on the shortlist (top three were nominated), missing the cutoff for finalist by the skin of my teeth. In fact, I was *so close* to being selected that the judging comittee needed one more day than planned to narrow down the finalists (MT told me a few weeks after champs). So yeah, stupid, avoidable code changes and what was probably one or two interview slip-ups. So close, yet so far; I just had to fuck up at the very last second... 

This thought stuck with me. At least, until two days ago, when I got a surprise text from MT that Ultro got off the advancement waitlist and qualified for the Western Edge Primer event. Sure, it's not worlds, but this event is not only the furthest that we've ever gotten in the team's ten year history, but also the first time we've officially qualified for a global event. Though I'm still very mad about all the championship slip-ups, this news is at least some confirmation that all the work I put into the robot earlier in the year actually set a new historical record for the team. 

That being said, Western Edge's timing is absolutely horrible. Firstly, I've barely worked on robotics since NYC championships, so I'll probably need to make several adjustments to my current off-season plan to make way for this event. Secondly, I have school exams literally the day after it ends (May 31st). I guess this is another reason why I hate being virtually the only programmer on the team. At this point, most of the improvements to our robot are software-related (e.g. *actually* doing patterns, consistent auto, etc.), so almost any change the team wants to make now is on my shoulder. If I were a senior right now, I wouldn't mind; after all, I like programming the robot. But when I have to juggle school exams, APs, piano, and squeeze whatever I can out of Ultro's robot at such a big event, things become overwhelming fast. 

I guess the bottom line right now is that I'm most likely going to Western Edge, but making very minimal changes to software if any. After all, I love California, and I think I have some obligation to support Ultro in this historical opportunity as one of its main contributors this season. But of course, Robotics isn't life; I need to prioritize to some extent at the end of the day. Oh well, I guess this is one of the really annoying things about going to such a small school.
