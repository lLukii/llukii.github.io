After NYC Championships, I got some insider information from both AM and MT in the days following champs, and it turns out the Ultro was on track for 3rd Place Inspire at one point (which could've very well solidified us a spot at worlds). Instead, a measly 10 lines of code changes ended up breaking our entire auto during the very matches that both judges and scouters watched. Subsequently, we walked away empty-handed without even making playoffs or any significant awards. The cherry on top was the FIRST leadership award finalist announcement: I was 4th on the shortlist (top three were nominated), missing the cutoff for finalist by the skin of my teeth. In fact, I was *so close* to being selected that the judging committee needed one more day than planned to narrow down the finalists (MT told me a few weeks after champs). Now, none of this information bothered me that much, since I'm passionate about robotics, and I know what I've done for the team; credentials are just a nice thing to have. But deep down, I still felt very salty that I was so incredibly close to obtaining *very* strong results, yet falling short at the very last second.


This thought stuck with me. At least, until two days ago, when I got a surprise text from MT that Ultro got off the advancement waitlist and qualified for the Western Edge Primer event in California. Sure, it's not worlds, but this event is not only the furthest that we've ever gotten in the team's ten-year history. Granted, I'm still very mad about all the championship slip-ups, and I don't think this event should make me or the team complacent about next year in any way. But, at the very least, this news is at least some confirmation that all the work I put into the robot earlier translated to something tangible in the end.


The bottom line is that I'm most likely going to Western Edge. After all, I love California, and I think I have some obligation to support Ultro in this historical opportunity. That being said, Western Edge's timing is absolutely horrible. Firstly, I've barely worked on robotics since NYC championships, so I'll probably need to make several adjustments to my current off-season plan to make way for this event. Secondly, I have school exams literally the day after it ends (May 31st). I guess this is another reason why I hate being virtually the only programmer on the team. At this point, most of our robot optimizations are software-related (e.g., *actually* doing patterns, consistent auto, etc.), so basically any functional change that the team wants to make now is on my shoulder. If I were a senior right now, I wouldn't mind; after all, I like programming the robot. But when I have to juggle school exams, APs, piano, and squeeze whatever I can out of Ultro's robot at such a big event, things become overwhelming fast.


I'm really swamped with work rn, so I don't know what else to say for now. I have some future blogs planned in mind, but I'll probably have to wait until after AP exams... alr cya
