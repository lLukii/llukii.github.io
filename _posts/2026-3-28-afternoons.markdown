So I noticed a pretty consistent trend with how my weekends, particularly Sundays, go:
* Mornings are great. I typically eat breakfast, maybe go to the park or grab a coffee, and enjoy the excitement of a new day. If I'm not seriously procrastinating, I would get some homework/other activities done. Otherwise, I'd read random blogs/Wikipedia articles/play a YouTube video in the background, but remain in a generally good mood.
* However, afternoons are when my problems usually kick in. If I was productive in the morning, I'd usually be tired around 2-3 PM and take a break. This usually entails going on Instagram and ~~rotting my brain~~, which leaves my afternoon very unproductive. By contrast, if I had spent the morning procrastinating, I'd usually feel the weight of delaying work, typically resulting in more anxiety and less motivation to do things. In short, Sunday afternoons are *very* sluggish.
* What's interesting to me, though, is that I tend to find evenings more endurable, even though I technically have *less* time until going to school the next day. I think this is mainly because my mind is a lot more tranquil at night, which helps me focus more on work instead of having to fight with other random-ass thoughts. It could just be that I'm a night person or that my ADHD kicks in during the afternoon. Idk.


I should also mention that one habit, subconsciously wired into my brain, is leaving certain forms of entertainment—such as playing PC games/watching anime, activities I'd consider to have "maximum entertainment value"—for the evenings. I think I do so to stop myself from getting too comfortable when I have stuff to get done, but it also makes procrastinating or taking a break during afternoons quite underwhelming. I guess this habit prevents me from getting distracted for too long, but I think it, in part, contributes to why afternoons feel so sluggish. I also have an issue with overexaggerating my workload, so I guess I'm just creating unnecessary stress for myself sometimes. I know, I'm hella paranoid sometimes...

Also, I dread Sunday afternoons, partly because I get reminded that I have school the next day. Ironically, this anxiety usually goes away at night, and I hardly find myself bored during Monday's first period Calc BC. Well, first-period APUSH is different, but my remaining classes for those days are really chill.


Sorry if this post feels kinda disorganized; I wrote it at an airport with some half-developed thoughts I wanted to unleash.
