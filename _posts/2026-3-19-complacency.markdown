Today, I learned a hard lesson that cramming for an AP Physics C test after not paying attention to class for two months was, indeed, a *terrible* idea. It's kinda funny because so many people in my class wanted a pass that the test was administered online 💀. Yeah, talk about cheating...


I was highkey tweaking during the FRQ section, making several really dumb mistakes. For one of the questions, I attempted to calculate torque with net force instead of friction, which made the rest of my work wrong. I somehow fakesolved my way to the right answer through a chain of algebraic mistakes, but my physics teacher, who's as qualified as Walter White teaching Chemistry, probably won't be so forgiving.


I was pretty mad after taking the test, realizing that this mistake, along with two others, could cost me a lot of points. My first instinct trying to cope with this L was claiming that these were sillies; I knew everything in circular motion and knew how to approach the problem. It was just a small mishap that caused me to sell the rest of the question. But, marking the words of [Kevin Zhou's blog on introductory physics](https://knzhou.github.io/writing/Advice.pdf), associating net force with torque is just like a chess player attempting to move his bishop like a knight. To an experienced chess player or a comfortable physics student, it's an unthinkable mistake.


At the end of the day, it's my fault that I hadn't really paid attention in physics until robotics season ended. Similarly, it's also my fault that my intuition of physics hasn't ameliorated enough for me to catch this error under time pressure. In other words, it's a skill issue—I have to bite the bullet, and take a (potentially) very bad test grade :/


It also doesn't help that skipping music theory classes is starting to take its toll. After being absent for two weeks for robotics, I went head-first into a midterm last week and legit didn't even know what the questions were asking 💀. Earlier today, I found out that I got an academic warning from my theory teacher. I guess I can really be a delinquent when I want to.


Oh well, I might as well give music theory a serious attempt now that I kinda have to. As a pianist for 10 years, it's about time that I can analyze a piece of music analytically, even though I don't feel like practicing and don't have any friends at MSM.
